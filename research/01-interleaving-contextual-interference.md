# 01 — Interleaving vs. blocked practice (contextual interference)

**Date:** 2026-07-05
**Question:** Should the app interleave keys/categories, or drill one thing at a
time in blocks? And *when* should interleaving kick in?

## Findings
- **Interleaved / random practice hurts *during* practice but improves *retention
  and transfer*** vs. blocked practice — the "contextual interference effect,"
  one of the most replicated results in motor learning.
- Illustrative study: elite baseball players given extra batting practice either
  blocked or interleaved — the interleaved group later hit ~57% of pitches vs.
  ~25% for the blocked group, despite performing worse during training.
- Mechanism: interleaving forces retrieval from long-term memory ("desirable
  difficulty") instead of keeping one rule in working memory.

## What this means for us
Interleaving is **beneficial and should stay** — the fix to "it moves on too soon"
is *not* to remove it. The refinement: **interleave keys that are already
mastered, not a batch of still-un-mastered ones.** Interleaving is the *reward*
for mastery, not the method of acquiring it. → Decision 01 (Adopted, Option B).
